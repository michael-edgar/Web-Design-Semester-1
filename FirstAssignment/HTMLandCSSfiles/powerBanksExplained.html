Portable Power Banks are comprised of a special battery in a special case with a special circuit to control power flow. They allow you to store electrical energy (deposit it in the bank) and then later use it to charge up a mobile device (withdraw it from the bank). Most power banks have two ports: a USB port for charging the mobile device and a micro USB port for charging the power bank itself. This means that if users have mobile device with a micro USB port (e.g. most Android phones), they only require one cable to charge the power bank and the mobile device.

Most power banks use the unit of electrical power mAh (Milli Amp Hour) .For more information about the mAh and why it's used click here

Power banks come in all shapes and sizes, so it can be hard to know which one to get. The big bulky expensive one? The small cheap but flimsy one? The one with a solar cell? To be perfectly honest, the type of power bank you require depends entirely on your situation. Some people may need a big bulky power bank because they may go for long periods of time without electricity or they may be likely to lose a smaller one. While others may only need a small power bank to top up their charge in the middle of the day. Some may not even need a power bank at all! To help you find the type of power bank that best suits you, here is a list of all types of power banks and how they may suit you:

Some power banks have extra features that bump up the price. For example, a built-in flashlight or solar cell, or maybe it is a reference to a popular game or tv show. Personally, I feel that while some of these features are very interesting, they often make the power bank cost more than they are worth, you could often get a similar power bank without the extra feature and something else with that feature for less much less than the power bank.

Like all technology, the power bank has also evolved over time. In the past ten or more years, the power bank has developed from a simple combination of a control circuit and a few AA batteries to more complex ones with multiple functions such as LED flashlight and wifi. The embryonic and development stage of the power bank was 2001-2009. It was first invented in the 2011 Las Vegas International Consumer Electronic Show. As some professional teams started to research and develop the core technology of the power bank it gained its precise definition: the framework of a power bank consists of mostly complete managing circuit, a chip and a battery.
